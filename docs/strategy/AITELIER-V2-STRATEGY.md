## Executive Summary

AI.TELIER undergoes a complete repositioning from "creative studio with soul" to **a foundational field for language, form, and work in the AI era** — inspired by the Bauhaus model but never explicitly naming it.

**Core Philosophy:** Futuro Ancestral (Ancestral Future)
> Technology that serves humans so naturally it seems to have always existed. Like fire. Like writing. Like language.

## Part 2: Council of Minds Analysis

### 2.1 Steve Jobs

**Key Insight:** "If you need to say you're Bauhaus, you're not Bauhaus."

**What Works:**
- "Technique became commodity. The invisible became the center."
- This alone could be the hero. Period. Nothing else.

**What to Fix:**
- Remove all explanations
- Site must be experience, not explanation
- Add environment before understanding

### 2.2 Walt Disney

**Key Insight:** "You have story ingredients but no emotional journey."

**What Works:**
- Three pillars (Studies, Studio, Archive) are solid
- Like Disney Method's three rooms

**What to Fix:**
- Add transformation arc
- Show, don't tell
- Add magic moments — scroll reveals that change something internally

### 2.3 Leonardo da Vinci

**Key Insight:** "Show the artifacts as proof of method."

**What Works:**
- Art + Science + Technology fusion
- Brutalism + Tech Ancestral is interesting synthesis

**What to Fix:**
- Visual Archive must be central, not lateral
- Define "Vorkurs" — the foundational curriculum
- Method must be visible

### 2.4 Kapil Gupta

**Key Insight:** "If you're afraid to repel, you're lying."

**What Works:**
- "Not service. It's field application."
- "The soul already exists. Our work is to reveal it."

**What to Fix:**
- Remove all guru promises ("invisible founders to inevitable authorities")
- Remove timeline promises ("3 weeks not 6 months")
- Real selectivity, not performative

### 2.5 Seth Godin

**Key Insight:** "Tribe before concept. Identity before product."

**What Works:**
- "Futuro Ancestral" is perfect Purple Cow
- Impossible to ignore paradox

**What to Fix:**
- Define "people like us"
- Create tribal identity before understanding
- Remove commodity racing (speed/price comparisons)

## Part 4: Copy Framework

### 4.1 Tone Guidelines

```yaml
tone:
  - Austere but not cold
  - Confident but not arrogant
  - Poetic but not flowery
  - Direct but not aggressive

structure:
  - Short sentences (3-7 words)
  - Paragraphs of 1-3 lines
  - Intentional repetition
  - Pauses (blank lines)

avoid:
  - Superlatives ("incredible", "revolutionary")
  - Result promises ("you will achieve")
  - Urgency language ("now", "last chance")
  - Price/time comparisons
  - Marketing jargon ("leverage", "scale")
  - Emojis (except Counselors, with purpose)

use:
  - Declarations ("It is so")
  - Negations that affirm ("Not an agency")
  - Paradoxes ("Futuro Ancestral")
  - Simple metaphors (fire, writing, film)
  - Invitations ("If this resonates")
```

## Part 5: Visual Direction

### 5.1 Brutalism + Futuro Ancestral

```yaml
aesthetic:
  base: "Digital Brutalism"
  temperature: "Cold with moments of warmth"

typography:
  titles: "Mono or heavy grotesque"
  body: "Humanist serif or clean sans"
  hierarchy: "Extreme - very large or very small"

colors:
  primary: "Deep black (#0a0a0a)"
  secondary: "Raw white (#f5f5f0)"
  accent: "Terracotta or amber (ancestral)"

grid:
  type: "Asymmetric, intentionally broken"
  whitespace: "Abundant - breathes"

images:
  treatment: "High contrast, grain, cinematic"
  ratio: "Cinema (2.35:1 or 16:9)"

movement:
  scroll: "Subtle reveals, not show-off"
  transitions: "Slow, intentional"

overall_vibe: |
  Contemporary art gallery
  meets 80s computer terminal
  meets zen temple.

  Technology present but invisible.
  Brutality with soul.
```

### 5.2 Specific Visual Elements

```
HERO
- Massive typography
- Lots of empty space
- No image or subtle background image (grain, blur)
- Minimal scroll indicator

SECTIONS
- Visible borders (exposed structure)
- Large section numbers (01, 02, 03)
- Short text blocks, much space between

IMAGES
- Full bleed when they appear
- Cinematic treatment
- Never decorative - always with purpose

BUTTONS
- Minimal
- Simple text in brackets [like this]
- Or discrete underline

FOOTER
- Almost nonexistent
- Only essentials
```
